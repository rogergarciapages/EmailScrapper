As a popular hub focused on efficient cross-chain swaps, Mixin Network seemed like a reliable player for asset trading.

That is until a cloud service hack in September ripped its customers off $200 million .

Shortly after the attack, Mixin hit the emergency brakes and suspended its entire network indefinitely.

According to the stunned Mixin team, they plan to refund up to 50% of stolen assets over time by issuing "tokenized liability claims" tied to potential future profits.

And in a desperate last-ditch attempt, Mixin even tried appeasing hackers with a $20 million bug bounty for returning funds - which ultimately went ignored.

So while Mixin picks up the pieces, its users face a long road to partially recover losses from one of 2023's largest decentralized platform exploits.
